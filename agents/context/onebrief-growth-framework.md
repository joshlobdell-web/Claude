# Onebrief Growth & Sales Framework

Source: Internal post from Nick (likely Nick Haberly, Army SDT ops manager), returning from field ~May 2026. Framework is data-driven sales execution applied to Onebrief's government customer base. Numbers are hypothetical until summer 2026 when actual data will be populated.

Purpose: move the organization from vibes-based selling to measurable, velocity-driven execution.

---

## Core Metrics

### 1. CAC — Customer Acquisition Cost
`CAC = (Sales Expenses + Marketing Expenses) / New Customers Acquired`

At Onebrief, CAC is dominated by HOG time and travel — no meaningful marketing spend. Approximate costs:
- Site visit: ~$3K
- Two trips/month per HOG: ~$6K/month

**Key risk:** Deals 12+ months in pipeline are sunk costs. Sales is zero-sum — an $18K travel spend on a dead command steals days from a motivated buyer. Deals are not free to lose.

**Gate check before booking a flight — all three must be confirmed:**
1. Budget available?
2. Internal champion identified?
3. Leadership engaged?

If any are unknown → send an email, not a flight.

---

### 2. LTV — Lifetime Value
`LTV = (Avg Revenue Per Account × Gross Margin %) / Churn Rate`

**Example:** $1M contract, continuous renewal = $5M LTV over five years. Year-1 churn = $1M LTV. Same acquisition cost, entirely different return.

**Government-specific headwinds:**
- Annual contracts mean every renewal is a re-sell.
- PCS cycle (1–3 years) makes champions temporary. If champion PCSs without a replacement identified, the account is at risk.

**Implication:** Poor customer fit is catastrophic because the clock resets every year. Honest qualification upfront equals revenue protection.

---

### 3. LTV:CAC Ratio
`LTV:CAC = Lifetime Value / Customer Acquisition Cost`

| Ratio | Signal |
|---|---|
| < 1:1 | Losing money |
| 3:1 | Healthy SaaS growth |
| 5:1+ | Underinvesting in sales |

**Applied:** Spending 10 site visits ($30K) + 6 months of HOG time to close a $1M contract that churns in year one does not work. The question is not just "can we win this?" It is: "if we win it, can we keep it, and does the acquisition cost make sense against that math?"

---

### 4. Pipeline Velocity ← Nick's primary growth metric
`Velocity = (Opportunities × Win Rate × Avg Deal Size) / Sales Cycle Length`

HOGs control every variable. Chasing volume without a filter is dangerous — a bloated pipeline means half the effort goes nowhere.

**Fast-close profile:**
- Motivated champion
- Engaged leadership
- Identified budget
- Clear use case

**Application:** Velocity tells you where to spend trips. Stop chasing commands with lukewarm leadership when a smaller command is ready to close in 30 days. Increase velocity by qualifying harder, not by adding volume.

---

### 5. ARPU — Average Revenue Per User
`ARPU = Total Recurring Revenue / Total Active Users`

**Example:** $1M / 100 active users = $10K per user annually. A typical command has ~800 viable users. Capturing 50 more = $500K addressable expansion in an account already owned.

**Application:** ARPU gives the CR team a concrete dollar figure to attach to user growth before walking into a renewal. Commands care about how their money is spent — translating adoption growth into dollars is a winning talking point.

---

### 6. DAU/MAU — Stickiness Factor ← Nick's primary CR metric
`Stickiness = Daily Active Users / Monthly Active Users`

| Pattern | Signal |
|---|---|
| Daily log-ins | Expansion asset |
| Monthly log-ins | Churn risk |

**Application:** CR reps must track this obsessively. "DAU/MAU is up 12 points, representing $200K in expansion value" is a fundamentally different renewal conversation than "the customer seems happy." End users are where stickiness lives — if they aren't logging in daily, the champion has nothing to point to at renewal.

## Buyer Roles

| Role | Who | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Champion** | J3, J5, or J4 | Pushed for Onebrief internally; goes to bat at renewal. Typically texting-terms relationship. If they PCS without a replacement identified, account is at risk. |
| **Decision Maker** | CoS, Commander | May never touch the product. Arm the champion to sell to them. |
| **Influencer** | Adjacent command, higher-echelon unit already on Onebrief, or a person who used it at their last assignment | Track these relationships even without an active deal. |
| **End User** | Planners and staff officers using the platform daily | Where DAU/MAU lives. No daily log-ins = champion has nothing to show at renewal. |
| **Gatekeeper** | Controls access to the Decision Maker | Never disrespect the gatekeeper. |
| **Buyer** | Budget/contracting types | Does not care about the product. Get the paperwork right. |

**Common failure mode:** HOG walks in assuming the J5 is the Decision Maker. The J5 is the Champion. The Chief of Staff is in the back of the room, unbriefed. The pitch was built for the wrong person.

**CCIR:** Champion and Decision Maker in the same room = closing opportunity. That specific meeting is CCIR — treat it as such.

---

## Framework Summary

| Metric | Controls | Watch for |
|---|---|---|
| CAC | Trip spend, HOG time | 12+ month pipeline = sunk cost |
| LTV | Renewal rate, champion continuity | PCS cycle resets the clock |
| LTV:CAC | Overall ROI of a deal | Win + churn = negative return |
| Pipeline Velocity | Where to spend trips | Bloated pipeline = wasted effort |
| ARPU | Expansion value in existing accounts | User growth = concrete renewal leverage |
| DAU/MAU | Stickiness, renewal readiness | Monthly log-ins = churn signal |
